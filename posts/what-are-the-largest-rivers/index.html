What are the largest rivers?

The largest river in the world is either the Amazon River or Nile River, depending on how you measure it. The Nile River is the longest on Earth while the Amazon River discharges the most amount of water per second in the world.

Which is the 6th largest river in the world?

6. Yellow River It is the 6th largest rivers on earth and the 2nd longest river in China. It has a length of approximately 5,464 km and a drainage area of 745,000 square kilometers and an average discharge of about 2,110 cubic meters per second.

Which is the second largest river in the world?

The Amazon of South America is the second longest river in the world and by far the largest by waterflow with an average discharge greater than the next seven largest rivers combined (not including Madeira and Rio Negro, which are tributuaries of The Amazon).

Which is the largest river in South America?

The Amazon River in South America passes through Brazil, Peru, and Columbia. At 3,997 miles long, it is not the longest river in the world, but it discharges an average of 7,831,000 cubic feet of water every second – more than the next seven largest rivers together. Water from the Amazon river makes up 20% of the world’s river flow.

Which is the largest river in North Africa?

Nile The Nile is a major north-flowing river in North Africa, generally regarded as the largest river in the world. It is 6,650 km (4,130 miles) long. It runs through the eleven countries of Sudan, South Sudan, Burundi, Rwanda, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Tanzania, Kenya, Ethiopia, Eritrea, Uganda and Egypt.
