What is the tallest mountain in Los Angeles?

Mount Baldy
Review: Mount Baldy The tallest mountain in Los Angeles county. Let’s start big picture. At 10,068 feet, Mount Baldy is the highest peak in the San Gabriel Mountains (and the highest point in Los Angeles County). The hike itself is about 6.5 miles long and climbs 2,300 feet.

Do I need a permit for Mt Baldy?

Wilderness permits are required for entry and are available free of charge from the Mt. Baldy Visitor Center and the San Gabriel River Ranger District Office. In order to protect the wilderness, quotas are in effect for areas within the San Bernardino portion of the Cucamonga Wilderness.

Is there a Mount Baldy in Colorado?

Mount Craig, also known as “Mount Baldy” due to its uncovered peak, sits on the shores of Grand Lake and reaches 12,007 feet in elevation. Along your journey to the top, you will see the headwaters of the Colorado River and Grand Lake, as well as various pristine alpine lakes that run along the base of the mountain.

Is Mt Baldy good for beginners?

Mount Baldy is not ideal for beginners, but 20 percent of the runs are beginner-level. This resort is mostly for intermediate and advanced skiers and snowboarders. Is Mt Baldy free?

Mt Baldy is also in the Angeles National Forest and requires an Adventure Pass for parking. These can be purchased in advance before reaching the mountain or once you pass through Mt Baldy Village. The pass is $5 for the day or $30 for a year (or even better, $35 for two cars for a year).
