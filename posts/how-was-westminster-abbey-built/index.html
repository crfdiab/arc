How was Westminster Abbey built?

Edward the Confessor built a new church on the site, which was consecrated on December 28, 1065. It was of considerable size and cruciform in plan. In 1245 Henry III pulled down the whole of Edward’s church (except the nave) and replaced it with the present abbey church in the pointed Gothic style of the period.

Why was the building of Westminster Abbey important?

Westminster Abbey was designed to display the finest characteristics of British history and architecture. As influences in architectural design changed, the Abbey developed a Gothic style of architecture, most prominently displayed in the towers. The chapter house was created to demand the presences of royal weddings, coronations, and burials.

When was the pipe organ installed in Westminster Abbey?

Tourists flock to marvel at Westminster Abbey’s gothic design, including its fan-vaulted ceilings and the magnificent pipe organ, installed for the coronation of King George VI in 1937. The organ contains some of the original piping of its predecessor instrument, which was built in 1848. There is also the Grave to the Unknown Warrior.

What kind of stained glass is in Westminster Abbey?
