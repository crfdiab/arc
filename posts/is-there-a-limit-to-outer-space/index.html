Is there a limit to outer space?

International law does not define the edge of space, or the limit of national airspace. The FAI defines the Kármán line as space beginning 100 kilometres (54 nautical miles; 62 miles; 330,000 feet) above Earth’s mean sea level.

Is it possible to travel beyond the observable universe?

What makes it impossible to know where space ends?

This one feature makes it almost impossible to know where space ends. You guessed it – it is expansion. From the day that all the dense matter blew up (do not worry if you do not remember this event, the Big Bang was 13.8 billion years ago), the universe started expanding.
