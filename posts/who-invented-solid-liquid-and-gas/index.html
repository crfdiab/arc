Who invented solid liquid and gas?

British scientist Joseph Priestley (1733–1804) made a key discovery when he isolated oxygen, a gas he called dephlogisticated air, in keeping with the theory of that time that a mysterious substance called phlogiston was in all substances.

Who first discovered states of matter?

The ancient Greeks were the first to identify three classes (what we now call states) of matter based on their observations of water.

Who discovered gas state?

The word gas was first used by the early 17th-century Flemish chemist Jan Baptist van Helmont. He identified carbon dioxide, the first known gas other than air.

Who was the scientist who called the gas oxygen an element?

The confusion of exactly what Priestley had discovered was solved by French chemist Antoine Laurent Lavoisier (1743–1794) who named the gas oxygen and called it an element, an element being by his definition a unique chemical substance. Priestley did not agree with Lavoisier.
