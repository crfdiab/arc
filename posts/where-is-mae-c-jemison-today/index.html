Where is Mae C Jemison today?

White Professor-at-Large at Cornell University in 1999. She went on to write her first book in 2001, Find Where the Wind Goes, which was a children’s book about her life. Currently, Jemison is leading the 100 Year Starship project through the United States Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA).

What happened to Mae Jemison 1992?

On September 12, 1992, Jemison finally flew into space with six other astronauts aboard the Endeavour on mission STS47, becoming the first African American woman in space. In recognition of her accomplishments, Jemison has received several awards and honorary doctorates.

How old was Mae Jemison when she was born?

In addition to her many awards, Jemison has been inducted into the National Women’s Hall of Fame and the International Space Hall of Fame. Mae Carol Jemison was born on October 17, 1956 in Decatur, Alabama.

What did Mae Jemison do after leaving NASA?

Jemison left NASA in 1993 after serving as an astronaut for six years in total. She started The Jemison Group, a consulting company that encourages science, technology, and social change. She also began teaching environmental studies at Dartmouth College and directed the Jemison Institute for Advancing Technology in Developing Countries.

When did Mae Jemison join the Peace Corps?

Shortly after her graduation, she became an intern at the Los Angeles County Medical Center, and then went on to practice general medicine. Fluent in Russian, Japanese and Swahili, Jemison joined the Peace Corps in 1983 and served as a medical officer for two years in Africa.

Where did Dr Mae Jemison live in Sierra Leone?
