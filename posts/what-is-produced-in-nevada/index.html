What does Nevada spend the most on?

The biggest areas of expenditures are in the core functions of health and human services (almost $11.5 billion, roughly 43.9 percent of the budget), education (nearly $6.6 billion, or 25.2 percent of budget), and infrastructure ($2.1 billion, around 8 percent of the budget).

Is there a state income tax in Nevada?

As is mentioned above, Nevada does not have a state income tax and no cities in the state levy local income taxes. Property taxes are also not a major source of financial concern for most Nevadans.
