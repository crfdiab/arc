Who Discovered Mount Logan?

Sir William E. Logan
Logan, Mount, elev 5959 m, is Canada’s highest mountain, named after Sir William E. Logan by Prof I.C. Russell, who first saw it during an attempted ascent of Mt St Elias in 1890.

How old are the rocks on Mount Logan?

They were deposited in a deep marine environment between 70 and 100 million years ago (Cretaceous period). These sedimentary rocks are juxtaposed with the older Mount Logan batholith along the Border Ranges Fault. Later, both rock types were intruded by granite.
