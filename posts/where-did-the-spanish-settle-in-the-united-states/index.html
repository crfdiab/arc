Who was the first person to settle in the United States?

The Spanish claim to territories that are today the United States rested upon the 16th century exploits of Ponce de Leon, Hernando De Soto, and Francisco V zquez de Coronado. How did Spain affect the history of the United States?

Although the Spanish elements in the history of the United States were mostly ignored by American historians in the decades after independence, the concept of the “Spanish borderlands” in the American Southwest was developed by American historians in the 20th century, which integrated Spain into U.S. history.

Why did the Spanish split South America in half?

South America was basically split in half between the Portuguese and the Spanish. Portugal had the massive land holding that was Brazil and the Spanish had basically the land surrounding Brazil to the West.

How did the Spanish colonize the Western Hemisphere?

Pope Alexander VI in a 4 May 1493 papal decree, Inter caetera, divided rights to lands in the Western Hemisphere between Spain and Portugal on the proviso that they spread Christianity. These formal arrangements between Spain and Portugal and the pope were ignored by other European powers.

When did the Spanish Empire start in the Americas?

The Spanish Empire began its interest in the Americas upon the arrival of Christopher Columbus in the Caribbean in 1492. The empire stretched across the Caribbean Islands, North America, Central America, and half of South America.

When did the Spanish take control of the Caribbean?

Beginning with the 1492 arrival of Christopher Columbus in the Caribbean and gaining control over more territory for over three centuries, the Spanish Empire would expand across the Caribbean Islands, half of South America, most of Central America and much of North America.
