Is Lake Whatcom polluted?

Lake Whatcom is a large natural lake in Whatcom County. The northwest end of the lake lies within the City of Bellingham, and 22 small watersheds drain into the lake. The lake has been threatened by declining water quality, and was put on the state’s list of polluted water bodies in 1998.

Is there bass in Lake Padden?

Lake Padden is a large freshwater lake located in Whatcom County, Washington. At an elevation of 450 ft. and 147.7 acres, it’s home to several species of fish, including kokanee, rainbow trout, largemouth bass and coastal cutthroat trout (resident). Lake Padden provides good shoreline access.
