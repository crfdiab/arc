Why are earthquakes are so common in Mexico?

Mexico’s location makes the country prone to strong earthquakes because it is in a so-called subduction zone. In Mexico’s case, an oceanic plate — the Cocos — is gradually sinking beneath a continental plate — the North American.

Where are the earthquake fault lines in Mexico?

Geology. Mexico lies within two seismically active earthquake zones. The Baja California peninsula lies near the boundary of the Pacific Plate and the North American Plate, while southern Mexico lies just north of the boundary between the North American Plate and the Cocos and Rivera tectonic plates.
