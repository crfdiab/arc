How do hurricanes cause tornadoes?

But as with any thunderstorm, the convective cells in a hurricane create strong updrafts. These can tilt the spinning air upright; a tornado is born. If the updraft is very strong, the spinning air will be packed tighter, with a smaller diameter.

What’s more dangerous a hurricane or tsunami?

A tsunami is large set of waves triggered by some sort of vertical movement of rocks under water (earthquake, large landslide, volcanic explosion). Both of them can be very severe when it comes to impacting humans, but overall hurricanes have probably caused more damage and deaths.
