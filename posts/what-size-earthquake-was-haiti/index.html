What size earthquake was Haiti?

magnitude 7.0
A magnitude 7.0 earthquake that struck Haiti on the afternoon of January 12, 2010, was one more disaster in a country that had suffered from decades of political, economic, and social setbacks and inequalities.

How big was the earthquake in Haiti in 2010?

2010 Haiti earthquake, magnitude 7.0 earthquake that struck some 15 miles (25 km) southwest of the Haitian capital of Port-au-Prince on January 12, 2010. Haiti’s government estimated that more than 300,000 were killed, but other estimates were considerably smaller. Hundreds of thousands of survivors were displaced.

What did people do in Haiti after the earthquake?

In the nights following the earthquake, many people in Haiti slept in the streets, on pavements, in their cars, or in makeshift shanty towns either because their houses had been destroyed, or they feared standing structures would not withstand aftershocks.

How many schools were destroyed in the Haiti earthquake?

About half the nation’s schools and the three main universities in Port-au-Prince were affected. More than 1,300 schools and 50 health care facilities were destroyed. The earthquake also destroyed a nursing school in the capital and severely damaged the country’s primary midwifery school.

What was the situation in Haiti in 2010?

November 2010: A girl who has contracted cholera lies on a cot at the hospital in Gonaives, a commune in northern Haiti and the capital of the Artibonite Region.
