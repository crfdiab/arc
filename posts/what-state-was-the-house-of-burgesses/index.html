What state was the House of Burgesses?

colonial Virginia
House of Burgesses, representative assembly in colonial Virginia, which was an outgrowth of the first elective governing body in a British overseas possession, the General Assembly of Virginia. The General Assembly was established by Gov. George Yeardley at Jamestown on July 30, 1619.

Who was known as the House of Burgesses?

With its origin in the first meeting of the Virginia General Assembly at Jamestown in July 1619, the House of Burgesses was the first democratically-elected legislative body in the British American colonies. About 140 years later, when Washington was elected, the electorate was made up of male landholders.

Who are the burgesses in the Maryland House of Delegates?

To the Lower House, freemen in each hundred elected burgesses as their representatives. From 1654, the county replaced the hundred as the local area represented. House of Delegates Chamber, State House, Annapolis, Maryland, January 2018.

Who made the House of Burgesses?

It was established by Virginia governor George Yeardley at Jamestown on July 30, 1619. The first colonial North American representative legislative body, the House of Burgesses was one division of the legislature established by Virginia governor George Yeardley at Jamestown, on July 30, 1619.

When did the House of Burgesses become the General Assembly?

When the Virginia colony declared its independence from the Kingdom of Great Britain at the Fifth Virginia Convention in 1776 and became the independent Commonwealth of Virginia, the House of Burgesses became the House of Delegates, which continues to serve as the lower house of the General Assembly.
