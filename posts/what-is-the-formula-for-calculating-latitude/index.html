What is the formula for calculating latitude?

Calculating the distance between latitude lines is easy because this distance never varies. If you treat the Earth as a sphere with a circumference of 25,000 miles, then one degree of latitude is 25,000/360 = 69.44 miles.

What is a latitude line?

Latitude is the measurement of distance north or south of the Equator. It is measured with 180 imaginary lines that form circles around the Earth east-west, parallel to the Equator. These lines are known as parallels. A circle of latitude is an imaginary ring linking all points sharing a parallel.

How to find the longitude and latitude of parcel corners?

You can do this from Google Maps or perhaps you have a plat map. Get a big black marker and write the longitude and latitude of each corner near that corner. Remember to write down the negative sign in front of longitude.
