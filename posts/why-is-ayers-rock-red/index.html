Why is Ayers Rock red?

Uluru is a type of rock called arkose. The flakes are bits of rock left after water and oxygen have decayed minerals in the rock. The red is the rusting of iron found naturally in arkose, and the grey is the rock’s original colour. You can see Uluru’s original grey inside many of its caves.

What makes Uluru in Australia red?

Over 550 million years ago, the rocks began to form and the erosion gave birth to the giant red monolith we see today. The result of this has been that the iron minerals found inside the arkose surface is rusting which ultimately led to its red colour. You may have heard that Uluru changes colours through the day.
