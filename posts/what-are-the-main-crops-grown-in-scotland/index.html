What are the main crops grown in Scotland?

Crops grown in Scotland include:

  • spring barley – the main crop.
  • winter wheat and winter barley.
  • oilseed rape, potatoes and other root crops – to a lesser extent.
  • soft fruit such as strawberries, raspberries and blackcurrants – grown mainly in Tayside and Fife.

How much money does the Scottish farming industry make?

When did agriculture start to take place in Scotland?

Agriculture in Scotland. Agriculture in Scotland includes all land use for arable, horticultural or pastoral activity in Scotland, or around its coasts. The first permanent settlements and farming date from the Neolithic period, from around 6,000 years ago.
