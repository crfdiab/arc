What word in English comes from Quechua?

The word karpa is its Quechua origin. Some South American countries have a more colloquial use of this word: Estar carpa (to be like a circus tent), a phrase with a very adult meaning.

What are 5 English words that come from Spanish?

Animals

  • alligator — el lagarto (“the lizard”)
  • armadillo — “little armored one”
  • barracuda — possibly from barraco (“snaggletooth”)
  • bronco — “rough”
  • burro — “donkey”
  • cockroach — anglicization of cucaracha.
  • mosquito — literally, “little fly”
  • mustang — mustango, from mesteño (“untamed”)

Is Puma a Quechua word?

The puma is a symbol of strength, wisdom and intelligence. It represents the ‘Kay Pacha’, Quechua word that means ‘the world of the living’. Its characteristics are patience and strength. It is believed that Cusco, the capital of the empire of the Incas, has the shape of a puma.

What are some of the Quechua words in Spanish?

In the Ecuadorian Spanish, you will find a considerable amount of Quechua words such as: cushqui (money), locro (a soup made of potatoes and cheese), guambra (a child), taita (dad), mucha (kiss), guagua (baby) and others.

What does the word Morocho mean in Quechua?

Morocho comes from the Quechua word muruch’u, which is a variety of hard corn. Its most common meaning in Spanish is a dark-skinned person. In some countries, it is used for people with fair skin but black hair. By extension, it also applies to animals with black skin.
