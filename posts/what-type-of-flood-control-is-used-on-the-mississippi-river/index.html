What type of flood control is used on the Mississippi river?

Levees
Traditional Levees Are Part of the Problem with Flooding Levees are the backbone of flood protection in the Mississippi River Basin. While they have helped prevent billions in damages, they also increase flood risk. Levees changed the Mississippi River by increasing river level and speed.

What methods have been employed to control flooding on the Mississippi river and Tributaries?

As a result, on the upper Mississippi and its tributaries, levees and floodwalls provide the bulk of the flood protection. Flood storage reservoirs exist on the tributaries to provide protection, primarily to communities on the tributaries. They are complemented by over 1600 km of local and private levees.

How are flood controlmethods used in flood relief?

Is the Mississippi River bound by levees or tributaries?

Much of the Lower Mississippi River is bound by levees, floodwalls and control structures as part of the Mississippi River & Tributaries (MR) project, a comprehensive, multi-state flood damage risk management program for the lower alluvial valley.
