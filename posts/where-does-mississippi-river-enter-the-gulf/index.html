Where does Mississippi River enter the Gulf?

The Mississippi River empties into the Gulf of Mexico about 100 miles (160 km) downstream from New Orleans. That area where the river empties into Gulf right off the coast of Louisiana is what’s called the Dead Zone. The size of the Dead Zone has reached larger sizes over the years concerning scientist and researchers.

Does the Mississippi River flow through Florida?

Fresh river water flowing from the Mississippi into the Gulf of Mexico does not mix into the salt water immediately. Instead, it stayed intact as it flowed through the Gulf of Mexico, into the Straits of Florida, and entered the Gulf Stream.
