How and why did Australia become part of the British Empire?

In 1770, Royal Navy James Cook, during his first voyage to the Pacific, sailed along and mapped the east coast of Australia, which he named New South Wales and claimed for Great Britain. The six colonies federated in 1901 and the Commonwealth of Australia was formed as a Dominion of the British Empire.

When did the British Empire invade Australia?

January 26, 1788
On January 26, 1788, Captain Arthur Phillip guides a fleet of 11 British ships carrying convicts to the colony of New South Wales, effectively founding Australia.

Why did people in Australia resent the British Empire?

Why was South Australia colonised by the British?

Why was the British Invasion of Australia a failure?

The systematic attempt to wipe out the Indigenous peoples of Australia by whatever means was doomed to failure given the tenacity, courage and underestimated strength of the people themselves. It has often been said that Australia was built on the backs of convict slave labour.
