What did Gouverneur Morris believe?

During the Constitutional Convention (1787), Morris advocated a strong central government, with life tenure for the president and presidential appointment of senators. As a member of the Committee of Style, he was largely responsible for the final wording of the Constitution.

How did Gouverneur Morris feel about slavery?

Views on slavery According to James Madison, who took notes at the Convention, Morris spoke openly against slavery on 8 August 1787, saying that it was incongruous to say that a slave was both a man and property at the same time: He [Morris] never would concur in upholding domestic slavery.

Did Gouverneur Morris want a strong central government?
