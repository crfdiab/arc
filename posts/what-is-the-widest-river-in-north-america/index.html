What is the widest river in North America?

Mississippi River
The Mississippi River is narrowest at its headwaters — between 20 and 30 feet (6 to 9 meters), according to the National Park Service (NPS). The river’s widest point — not counting some lakes in Minnesota and Wisconsin — is just downstream from its confluence with the Missouri River, near Alton, Illinois.
