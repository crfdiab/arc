What type of government does each country in Asia has?

In South Asia, five countries have parliamentary governments, including Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal and Pakistan. Of these, three are federal republics (India, Nepal and Pakistan), one is a unitary republic (Bangladesh) and one is a constitutional monarchy (Bhutan).

What type of economy does Asia have?

The economies of Japan and South Korea are Western-style, free-market, capitalist economies. The economy of China is known as a socialist market economy, which involves a dominant state-owned sector, operating in an open-market economy.

What are some of the types of government in Asia?

The countries of Asia feature a number of different types of government, the majority being republics of one type or another. These include democratic, theocratic and federal republics. In addition, there are countries with single-party governments, constitutional monarchies and absolute monarchies in the continent.
