What animals live in the canopy of the Amazon?

Under the canopy and on tall branches there are monkeys, slothes with large, strong nails they use to hang from branches, small birds feeding on nectar (hummingbirds in America, sunbirds in Africa), many-coloured birds (toucans, parrots, birds of paradise), arboreal snakes, large butterflies, bats (flying foxes).

What type of animals live in the canopy layer?

The canopy layer is teeming with life as it is rich in fruits and seeds. Animals such as insects, birds, monkeys, frogs and sloths are found here. It is said the canopy layer is home to 90% of animals in the rainforest.

How old are the trees in the Amazon rainforest?

In some usages, the definition of a tree may be narrower, including only woody plants with secondary growth, plants that are usable as lumber or plants above a specified height. In looser senses, the taller palms, the tree ferns, bananas and bamboos are also trees. Trees tend to be long-lived, some reaching several thousand years old.

Why are the trees of the Amazon important?

The phrase refers to the significant amount of oxygen produced by the trees and other plant life of the Amazon. The Amazon basin and South America have a vast array of incredible trees.
