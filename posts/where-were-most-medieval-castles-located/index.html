Where were castles built and what did they serve as?

Medieval castles were built as homes for kings and nobility, the noble class of a country such as royalty and important public figures, to protect them from unwanted intruders. Castles were typically built in the middle of the land the king or lord of the castle was ruler over.

What kind of castles were built in medieval times?

The motte-and-bailey castle eventually lost its popularity, and was replaced by a new type of castle. Although motte-and-bailey castles were still being built during the 11 th century, a new type of castle, i.e. the stone keep castle, was emerging in the same century.
