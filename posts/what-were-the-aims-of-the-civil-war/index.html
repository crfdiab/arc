What were the aims of the Civil War?

The Union war effort expanded to include not only reunification, but also the abolition of slavery. To achieve emancipation, the Union had to invade the South, defeat the Confederate armies, and occupy the Southern territory.

What was the agreement of the Civil War?

There was no treaty signed to end the Civil War. The surrender at Appomattox Court House was a military surrender of an army which was surrounded. The Confederate government never surrendered and even had it wanted to the United States government would likely not have accepted.

Was there a general Main in the Civil War?

There were hundreds of generals commissioned in the American Civil War on both the Union and Confederate armies. Some, like Robert E. Lee, Stonewall Jackson, Ulysses S. Grant and William Tecumseh Sherman are household names.

What was the original purpose of fighting the Civil War?

In the South, most slaves did not hear of the proclamation for months. But the purpose of the Civil War had now changed. The North was not only fighting to preserve the Union, it was fighting to end slavery. Throughout this time, northern black men had continued to pressure the army to enlist them.

What was Lincoln’s role in the Civil War?

Role of Abraham Lincoln during American Civil War Introduction Abraham Lincoln was the Commander in Chief during the time of the American Civil War, successfully prosecuted the Civil War, which initially aims to preserve the Union turned as a fight to liberate the oppressed.

How did General Grant and General Lee end the Civil War?

When Generals Grant and Lee sat down at Appomattox Court House, they brought an end to the struggle that had consumed the nation for five long years The two commanders sat across from each other; Lee in a tall caned armchair and Grant in a swivel chair with a padded leather back next to a small oval side table.
