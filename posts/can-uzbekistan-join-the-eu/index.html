Where are the borders of Uzbekistan in the world?

Uzbekistan has borders with five states. In the north-west Uzbekistan borders on Kazakhstan, from the northeast – on Kyrgyzstan, the south-eastern part adjoins to Tajikistan, from the south-west – Turkmenistan, and a small part of the territory in the south has borders with Afghanistan.

When did Uzbekistan become part of the Russian Empire?

It was gradually incorporated into the Russian Empire during the 19th century, with Tashkent becoming the political center of Russian Turkestan. In 1924, after national delimitation, the constituent republic of the Soviet Union known as the Uzbek Soviet Socialist Republic was created.
