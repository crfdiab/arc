Is the Mediterranean Sea the largest sea in Europe?

The Mediterranean Sea – With an area of 2.5 million sq. km, the Mediterranean Sea is one of the largest seas in the world that is bordered by the continents of Europe in the north, Africa in the south, and Asia in the east. In the west, the sea is connected with the Atlantic Ocean via the narrow Strait of Gibraltar.

Which is the westernmost part of the Mediterranean Sea?

The Alboran Sea – The Alboran Sea is the westernmost portion of the Mediterranean Sea that is situated between the Iberian Peninsula and the northern part of Africa. The sea is bordered by the British Overseas Territory of Gibraltar in the west; by Spain in the north and by Algeria and Morocco in the south.

Which is the narrowest strait between Asia and Europe?

Bosphorus or Bosporus Strait – Also referred to as the Istanbul Strait, the Bosporus Strait is a body of water that forms a part of the boundary between the continents of Asia and Europe. Located in northwestern Turkey, Bosporus Strait is one of the world’s narrowest straits that connect the Black Sea with the Sea of Marmara.
