Is February a good time to visit Australia?

The best times to visit Sydney are September through November and from March to May. Thanks to Australia’s location in the Southern Hemisphere, Sydney’s warmest weather and peak tourist season falls between December and February (winter in the Northern Hemisphere).

What is the weather like in Australia in January and February?

Tasmania and Hobart experience four distinct seasons. Its climate is described as mild temperate maritime. The warmest months are December, January, February and March with average temperatures of around 21°C/70°F With an annual average of 626 millimetres of rainfall, Hobart is Australia’s second-driest capital city.

Is February cold in Australia?

Climate outlook overview temperatures for February to April are likely to be warmer than average over Tasmania and around much of the Australian coastline. Overnight temperatures for February to April are very likely to be above average across nearly all of Australia.

How hot is Melbourne in February?

around 78°F
Daily high temperatures are around 78°F, rarely falling below 66°F or exceeding 93°F. The highest daily average high temperature is 79°F on February 4. Daily low temperatures are around 58°F, rarely falling below 50°F or exceeding 67°F.

What is the best month to go to Australia?

What is the best time to visit Australia? September to November & March to May are best time to visit Australia. Most of the tourists opt for these months to visit Australia. The weather during these seasons is neither too hot nor too cold that makes it perfect to have a walk around Australia.

What season is February in Australia?

In Australia, the seasons are defined by grouping the calendar months in the following way: Spring – the three transition months September, October and November. Summer – the three hottest months December, January and February. Autumn – the transition months March, April and May.

Is February going to be hot in Melbourne?

Summer (December – February) These months are dry, with occasional hot spells that can last more than three days. Melbourne’s top temperatures are usually in January and February, when temperatures can occasionally soar past 30°C (86°F).
