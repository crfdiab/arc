How did the United States stay neutral during the French Revolution?

The United States thus remained formally neutral despite heavy pressure from both sides. The political activities of French citizens in the United States and the proliferation of spies led the Federalists, who controlled Congress, to pass the Alien and Sedition Acts in 1798.

Why did the Federalists support the French Revolution?

Conservatives like Hamilton, Washington, and others who would soon organize as the Federalist political party saw the French Revolution as an example of homicidal anarchy.
