What is the difference between New Guinea and Papua New Guinea?

New Guinea is an island to the north of the Australian mainland, south of the equator. Politically, the western half of the island comprises two provinces of Indonesia: Papua and West Papua. The eastern half forms the mainland of the country of Papua New Guinea.
