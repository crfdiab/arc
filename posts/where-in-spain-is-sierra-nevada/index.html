Where in Spain is Sierra Nevada?

province of Granada
Sierra Nevada (Spanish: [ˈsjera neˈβaða]; meaning “mountain range covered in snow”) is a mountain range in the region of Andalucia, in the province of Granada and, a little further, Málaga and Almería in Spain. It contains the highest point of continental Spain: Mulhacén, at 3,479 metres (11,414 ft) above sea level.

Where are the high Sierra Mountains located?

eastern California
The High Sierra is in eastern California, USA, in the Sierra Nevada mountain range. The primary area of interest to the HSHA and its members is the 200-mile (320 km) long Sierra crest from Lake Tahoe to the Golden Trout Wilderness (south of Sequoia National Park).

Is it High Sierra or high Sierras?

The Sierra Nevada is distinctly a unit, both geographically and topographically, and is well described as “una sierra nevada.” Strictly speaking, therefore, we should never say “Sierras,” or “High Sierras,” or “Sierra Nevadas” in referring to it.
