What was the College of New Jersey called?

Princeton University
1387. A: The College of New Jersey, founded in 1746, changed its name to Princeton University during the culmination of the institution’s Sesquicentennial Celebration in 1896. Historically, the University was often referred to as “Nassau,” “Nassau Hall,” “Princeton College,” or “Old North.”

How many community colleges are there in New Jersey?

New Jersey has a system of 18 public community colleges at the county level statewide. This reflects the fact that each college serves one of New Jersey’s 21 counties, except for Atlantic Cape Community College and Raritan Valley Community College, each of which serves two counties.
