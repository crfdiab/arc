Who was the first person to discover New York?

European discovery of New York was led by the Italian Giovanni da Verrazzano in 1524 followed by the first land claim in 1609 by the Dutch. As part of New Netherland, the colony was important in the fur trade and eventually became an agricultural resource thanks to the patroon system.

Who was the first European explorer to explore North America?

1497 – Under the commission of Henry VII of England, Italian explorer John Cabot discovers Newfoundland, becoming the first European to explore the coast of mainland North America since the Norse explorations of Vinland five centuries earlier. 1497–98 – Vasco da Gama sails to India and back.

When did Giovanni da Verrazzano explore New York?

In 1524, Giovanni da Verrazzano, an Italian explorer in the service of the French crown, explored the Atlantic coast of North America between the Carolinas and Newfoundland, including New York Harbor and Narragansett Bay. On April 17, 1524 Verrazzano entered New York Bay, by way of the Strait now called the Narrows.
