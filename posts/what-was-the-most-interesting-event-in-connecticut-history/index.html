Who was involved in the settlement of Connecticut?

Dutch explorer Adriaen Block sails along the Connecticut coastline and up the Connecticut River. The Earl of Warwick signs the “Warwick Patent,” a deed of conveyance granting land rights in what is now southeastern Connecticut.

What was the population of Bridgeport Connecticut in 1950?

Bridgeport’s growth accelerated even further from the mid-19th century to the mid-20th century with the advent of the railroad, Industrialization, massive immigration, labor movements until, at its peak population in 1950, Bridgeport with some 159,000 people was Connecticut’s second most populous city.

Kids learn about the history and timeline of the state of Connecticut including early explorers, Native Americans, colonial settlers, the Revolutionary War, and the Civil War.

What was the first newspaper in the Connecticut Colony?

1755–Connecticut Gazette of New Haven, the Colony’s first newspaper, printed by James Parker at New Haven. 1763–Brick State House erected on New Haven Green. 1764–Connecticut Courant, the oldest American newspaper in continuous existence to the present, launched at Hartford by Thomas Green.
