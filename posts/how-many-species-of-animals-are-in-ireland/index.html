How many species of animals are in Ireland?

Ireland is home to a variety of different species of land and sea mammals, birds, plants and insects. There are approximately 50 species of land mammals e.g. hedgehog, pine martin, badger and sea mammals e.g. seals, dolphin and whales. We have over 400 species of birds and more than 4,000 plant species.

How did animals get to Ireland?

Only 26 land mammal species (including bats, but not including marine mammals) are native to Ireland, because it has been isolated from the European mainland (by rising sea levels after the Midlandian Ice Age), since about 14,000 BC. In addition, ten species of bat are found in Ireland.

What is the biggest mammal in Ireland?

Deer are Ireland’s largest land mammal.

How many species of mammals live in Ireland?

34-ish mammals in Ireland. Some sources claim that 35 species of land mammal live on Ireland’s land and around our coast (an example of a coastal land mammal is a seal). Others (me) say 34 species, still others (ahem… wikipedia) claim only 31. Well there’s only one thing for it, a list.

Is it good to study mammals in Ireland?

The study of Irish mammals carries both advantages and disadvantages for amateur and professional naturalist alike. A would-be ornithologist must undergo an arduous apprenticeship in learning to identify accurately even the common Irish bird species, simply because there are so many, and the variations between them often slight and subtle.

When was the first introduced species in Ireland?

The Irish population originated from a single introduction in 1911, at Castle Forbes in Co. Longford. In general the longer an introduced species has been in the country the better we accept it.

Is there a marine mammal database in Ireland?
