How is the word traffic derived?

1500, “trade, commerce,” from French trafique (15c.), from Italian traffico (14c.), from trafficare “carry on trade,” of uncertain origin, perhaps from a Vulgar Latin *transfricare “to rub across,” from Latin trans “across” (see trans-) + fricare “to rub” (see friction), with the original sense of the Italian verb …

Where does the word the originate from?

It is derived from gendered articles in Old English which combined in Middle English and now has a single form used with pronouns of any gender. The word can be used with both singular and plural nouns and with a noun that starts with any letter.

What does the term traffic means?

1 : the movement (as of vehicles or pedestrians) along a route. 2 : the people or goods carried by train, boat, or airplane or passing along a road, river, or air route. 3 : the business of carrying passengers or goods the tourist traffic.

Is Trafficky a real word?

What is road traffic meaning?
