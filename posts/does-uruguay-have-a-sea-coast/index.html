Does Uruguay have a sea coast?

Uruguay is a country in the southeastern region of South America, bordering the Atlantic Ocean, between Argentina and Brazil. It is bordered to the west by Argentina, on the north and northeast by Brazil, and on the southeast by the Atlantic Ocean, which makes up Uruguay’s coast.

Does Uruguay and Bolivia have oceans?

Bolivia and Paraguay remain the only countries in Latin America with no direct access to sea.

What kind of beaches are there in Uruguay?

The beaches are beautiful, but the water is cold. Most of the beaches in Uruguay sit on the southern Atlantic Ocean, which is not known for its balmy waters. Others are situated on the Rio de la Plata, which is famous for being the widest river in the world but certainly not the most swimmable.

Where are the Rio de la Plata and Uruguay located?

The littorals of the Río de la Plata and the Río Uruguay are somewhat broader and merge more gradually into the hilly interior. The remaining two-quarters of the country is a rolling plateau marked by ranges of low hills that become more prominent in the north as they merge into the highlands of southern Brazil.
