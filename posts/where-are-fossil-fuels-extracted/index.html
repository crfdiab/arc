Where are fossil fuels extracted?

Coal. The majority of all mining for fossil fuels involves the extraction of coal. Coal can be extracted close to the upper portion of the earth’s crust, called surface mining, or from deep within the earth through underground mining.

Which fossil fuel is the hardest to extract?

Where can coal be extracted from the Earth?

Coal can be extracted close to the upper portion of the earth’s crust, called surface mining, or from deep within the earth through underground mining. Recovering coal through surface mining is relatively easy; shovels and bulldozers are effective at extracting coal near the surface.
