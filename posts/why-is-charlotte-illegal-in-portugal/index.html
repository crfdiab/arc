Why is Charlotte illegal in Portugal?

Portugal: No nicknames allowed This means that Portuguese parents keen on Princess Charlotte’s name will have to look elsewhere for inspiration. Another rule is that babies must be given full names – with no nicknames or abbreviations. This means you can call a baby Thomas or Catherine, but not Tom or Kate.

Why is the name Chloe banned in Iceland?

Icelandic parents have some seriously strict requirements when it comes to baby names. Names must fit with Icelandic grammar, so any name with a C, Q or W in it is banned. This means there are no babies called Bruce, Charlotte, Chris, Chloe, William or Jack.
