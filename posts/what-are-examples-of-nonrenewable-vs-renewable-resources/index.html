What are examples of nonrenewable vs renewable resources?

Fossil fuels such as oil, natural gas, and coal are examples of nonrenewable resources. Humans constantly draw on the reserves of these substances while the formation of new supplies takes eons. Renewable resources are the opposite: Their supply replenishes naturally or can be sustained.

The two broad categories of non-renewable resources are fossil fuels and nuclear energy (from uranium ore).

    Why is coal considered a non renewable resource?

    Coal is considered as non-renewable resource because we can’t replicate the environment (Very high temperature and pressure) in which it was initially formed. Plus, it takes millions of years for it to be produced in the first place!

    Why is uranium considered a non renewable resource?

    It is a non-renewable resource because of its cosmic origin. The isotopes of uranium were formed 6.6 billion years ago in supernovas and do not naturally regenerate. In fact, the radioactive decay of the natural deposits of uranium is what keeps the earth’s core in a liquid form [13].
