What did Operation Ocean Shield do?

Operation Ocean Shield focused on protecting the ships of Operation Allied Provider, which transported relief supplies as part of the World Food Programme’s mission in the region. The initiative also helped strengthen the navies and coast guards of regional states to assist in countering pirate attacks.

Is Somalia a NATO?

With the consent of Somali authorities, NATO vessels were able to also enter the territorial waters of Somalia. Operations on Somali land were not part of the NATO mandate.

What is anti-piracy operation?

Since 2008, IN has been regularly deployed for anti-piracy missions in the Gulf of Aden along with other navies of the world so as to safeguard maritime trade and curb piracy in the region. As on 25 Sep 17, 65 IN ships have been deployed on anti-piracy patrol duties.

Why did Operation Ocean Shield end?

NATO has ended Operation Ocean Shield after a sharp drop-off in attacks by Somali pirates. The Royal Danish Air Force carried out the last Indian Ocean surveillance missions for NATO. During its peak, piracy off the Horn of Africa had an economic impact of $7 billion, with more than 1,000 hostages taken.
