How are borders divided?

Borders are political boundaries. They separate countries, states, provinces, counties, cities, and towns. A border outlines the area that a particular governing body controls. The government of a region can only create and enforce laws within its borders.

What type of boundaries does the US have?

The United States shares international land borders with two nations: The Canada–United States border to the north of the Contiguous United States and to the east of Alaska. The Mexico–United States border to the south.

Which type of boundaries are the officially drawn dividing lines between nations states cities and so on?

Political boundaries are the officially drawn dividing lines between nations, states, cities and so on. Political boundaries are often formed by physical boundaries.
