When was Huber Heights Ohio established?

The township was formed on January 1, 1810, and was named after Major General Anthony Wayne. In 1981, the town was renamed Huber Heights and was incorporated as a city. This name was in honor of Charles Huber, who developed the first track of brick homes in this area and founded a utility company in Ohio in 1956.

How did Huber Heights develop?

In 1956 Charles H. Huber started the first privately owned utility company in Ohio and launched the construction of his first plat of brick, single-family homes. Over the period 1956-1992, Huber Homes built a total of 10,707 single-family homes and 2,258 multi-family units in the community.
