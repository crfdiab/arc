What caused the final Chinese empire to collapse?

After the deaths of the Guangxu Emperor and Cixi in 1908, Manchu conservatives at court blocked reforms and alienated reformers and local elites alike. The Wuchang Uprising on 10 October 1911 led to the Xinhai Revolution. The abdication of Puyi, the last emperor, on 12 February 1912, brought the dynasty to an end.

What destroyed the Chinese empire?

On October 10, 1911, three years after the death of the Dowager, the Xinhai Revolution, also known as the “1911 Revolution”, broke out against the Manchu rulers. Spreading from province to province, the revolution fell the Qing monarchy in just four months.

Why did China collapse so much?

Chinese historians have spent well over a thousand years trying to understand why the Han Dynasty collapsed. Over time they developed three main theories: 1) bad rulers; 2) the influence of empresses and court eunuchs over child emperors too young to rule by themselves; and 3) the Yellow Turban Revolt.
