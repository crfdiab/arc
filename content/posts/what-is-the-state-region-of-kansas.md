How did the city of Topeka get its name?
----------------------------------------

The name Topeka is a Kansa-Osage sentence that means “place where we dug potatoes”, or “a good place to dig potatoes”. As a placename, Topeka was first recorded in 1826 as the Kansa name for what is now called the Kansas River.

Where is the capital of the state of Kansas?
--------------------------------------------

Topeka ( / toʊˈpiːkə /; Kansa: Tó Pee Kuh) is the capital city of the U.S. state of Kansas and the seat of Shawnee County. It is along the Kansas River in the central part of Shawnee County, in northeast Kansas, in the Central United States. As of the 2010 census, the city population was 127,473.
