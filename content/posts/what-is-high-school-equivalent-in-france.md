What is high school equivalent in France?
-----------------------------------------

After the collège students attend the lycée (high school) for the final three years of secondary education. Students then take an examination to receive the baccalauréat (bac) qualification. The baccalauréat is equivalent to the US high school diploma, but differs in that it requires preparatory study.

Is the French school system harder?
-----------------------------------

There is little leeway for a foreign child adapting to the French education system: it is done the school’s way or not at all. The primary years in a French school can be very challenging but also stimulating and rewarding. Very little learning support exists for the able, or less able, child.

What kind of schools do they have in France?
--------------------------------------------

French education is divided into primary level ( école ), elementary or middle school ( collège) and high school ( lycée ). To read about the French education system, see Expatica’s guide to the school system in France.
