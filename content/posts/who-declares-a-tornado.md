WHO declares a tornado?
-----------------------

Tornado Warning They are usually issued for a duration of around 30 minutes. A Tornado Warning is issued by your local National Weather Service office (NWFO), see map below. It will include where the tornado was located and what towns will be in its path.

How does the tornado alarm work?
--------------------------------

The warnings are broadcast from cell towers in the vicinity of the tornado and flash flood, so you will receive them if you are near the hazard. A NOAA Weather Radio receiver will sound an alarm to alert you when a storm warning is issued. The receivers needed to receive the alarm are available at electronics stores.

Why do tornado sirens go off?
-----------------------------

The tornado sirens are activated with a signal from the NWS that indicates a tornado has been spotted or is strongly indicated on a Doppler radar and/or strong, damaging winds of 70 mph or greater are present. The alert goes off in the specific cities or towns that will be affected, not throughout the entire county.
