What resources did Prince Edward Island have?
---------------------------------------------

Our economy features a mix of traditional resources – agriculture, fisheries and tourism – that have undergone significant modernization as well as new industry sectors including bioscience and advanced manufacturing. Business acumen and entrepreneurship are at the core of PEI’s economic success.

What is mined in Prince Edward Island?
--------------------------------------

In the area between Dartmouth and the Strait of Canso, there are numerous old gold mines and a few copper, lead, tungsten, and iron mines. Good specimens of specularite, siderite, arsenopyrite, and andalusite can be collected.

What is the traditional food of Prince Edward Island?
-----------------------------------------------------

Prince Edward Island is famous for its oysters, mussels and even potatoes, and now visitors can enjoy “field to table” culinary touring with a range of hands-on food experiences.

When did the British take over Prince Edward Island?
----------------------------------------------------

The British claimed dominion over all of the Maritimes in 1763. A separate colony on Prince Edward, named “St. John’s Island” was established on June 28, 1769, after determined lobbying by the island’s settlers. During the American Revolutionary War, Charlottetown was raided in 1775 by a pair of American-employed privateers.

Why is Prince Edward Island called the birthplace of Confederation?
-------------------------------------------------------------------

As a result of having hosted the inaugural meeting of Confederation, the Charlottetown Conference, Prince Edward Island presents itself as the “Birthplace of Confederation” with several buildings, a ferry vessel, and the Confederation Bridge, the longest bridge over ice-covered waters in the world, using the term “confederation” in many ways.
