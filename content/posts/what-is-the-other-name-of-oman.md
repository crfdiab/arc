What is the old name of Muscat?
-------------------------------

Muscat, Arabic Masqaṭ, town, capital of Oman, located on the Gulf of Oman coast. The town long gave its name to the country, which was called Muscat and Oman until 1970.

Where did the country of Oman get its name?
-------------------------------------------

The origin of Oman’s name is uncertain. It seems to be related to Pliny the Elder ‘s Omana and Ptolemy ‘s Omanon ( Ὄμανον ἐμπόριον in Greek ), both probably the ancient Sohar.

Who is the head of State in Oman?
---------------------------------

The government system of Oman is a Sultanate, an Arab monarchy. Chief of state and head of government is the sultan, he serves also as the Prime Minister, the head of the defence and the finance ministries, and is the chairman of the central bank.

When did the Portuguese take Muscat from Oman?
----------------------------------------------

The Portuguese took Muscat on 1 April 1515, and held it until 26 January 1650, although the Ottomans controlled Muscat from 1550 to 1551 and from 1581 to 1588. In about the year 1600, Nabhani rule was temporarily restored to Oman, although that lasted only to 1624 with the establishment of the fifth imamate, also known as the Yarubid Imamate.
