James Schoolcraft Sherman (October 24, 1855 – October 30, 1912) was an American politician who was a United States representative from New York from 1887 to 1891 and 1893 to 1909, and the 27th vice president of the United States from 1909 until his death in 1912.

What did General Sherman do after the Civil War?
------------------------------------------------

Sherman opened a prominent law office in Mansfield, Ohio and invested heavily in railroads and real estate. President Lincoln appointed Sherman the military commandant of military facilities at Mansfield and later as commissioner of war claims in St Louis.
