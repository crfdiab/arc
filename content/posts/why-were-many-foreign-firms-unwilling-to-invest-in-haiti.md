Why were many foreign firms unwilling to invest in Haiti?
---------------------------------------------------------

Haiti prohibited foreign investment and foreign property ownership, but foreigners were uninterested in investing in this nation after a slave revolt. Due to these factors, as well as the desires of the ex-slaves, the economy began to focus on subsistence farming rather than plantation agriculture.

What were the two immediate economic problems Haiti faced?
----------------------------------------------------------

First, the warfare of the Haitian Revolution destroyed the capital and infrastructure of the economy. Second, Haiti lacked diplomatic and trade relations with other nations. Third, Haiti lacked investment, both foreign and domestic investment.

What are the most serious economic problems facing Haiti?
---------------------------------------------------------

Poverty, corruption, vulnerability to natural disasters, and low levels of education for much of the population represent some of the most serious impediments to Haiti’s economic growth.

What are the advantages and disadvantages of Haiti?
---------------------------------------------------

The vegetation is rich, providing broadleaf forests. This can be beneficial because such nature can become a major tourist attraction. Another benefit from the vegetation is the development of crops. Crops, both edible and cash crops, can be used for export.

Why are there so many natural disasters in Haiti?
-------------------------------------------------

Haiti is along the Ring of Fire. This is a line of active underwater volcanoes. When these submerged volcanoes erupt, the surface of the water and any nearby lands are affected. For example, tsunamis commence and earthquakes shake territories to oblivion. Earthquakes and tsunamis aren’t the only natural disasters amongst Haiti.
