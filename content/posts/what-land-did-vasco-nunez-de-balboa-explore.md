What land did Vasco Nunez de Balboa explore?
--------------------------------------------

The 16th-century Spanish conquistador and explorer Vasco Núñez de Balboa (1475-1519) helped establish the first stable settlement on the South American continent at Darién, on the coast of the Isthmus of Panama. In 1513, while leading an expedition in search of gold, he sighted the Pacific Ocean.

Why did Vasco Nunez de Balboa go to Darien?
-------------------------------------------

In 1513, Balboa led an expedition from Darién to search for a new sea reportedly to the south and for gold. He hoped that if he was successful, he would win the favor of Ferdinand, the king of Spain. Why did Vasco Nunez de Balboa name the Bay San Miguel?
------------------------------------------------------

After traveling more than 110 km (68 mi), Balboa named the bay where they ended up San Miguel, because they arrived on September 29, the feast day of the archangel Michael. He named the new sea Mar del Sur, since they had traveled south to reach it.

Who was the first Spanish explorer to see the Pacific Ocean?
------------------------------------------------------------

Vasco Nuñez de Balboa was a Spanish explorer, governor, and conquistador. He is best known for leading an expedition across the narrow isthmus of Panama, and reaching the Pacific Ocean, becoming the first European to see this large body of water.

Why was Vasco Nunez de Balboa executed in 1519?
-----------------------------------------------
