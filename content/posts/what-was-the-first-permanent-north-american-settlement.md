What was the first permanent North American settlement?
-------------------------------------------------------

Jamestown, Virginia  
In 1607, 104 English men and boys arrived in North America to start a settlement. On May 13 they picked Jamestown, Virginia for their settlement, which was named after their King, James I. The settlement became the first permanent English settlement in North America.

What was the first name of North Carolina?
------------------------------------------

Carolus  
North Carolina is named in honor of King Charles I of England who first formed the English colony, Carolus being Latin for “Charles”….

What was the first permanent settlement in the United States?
-------------------------------------------------------------

A: Jamestown, Va., was the first permanent English settlement in the United States. It was founded in 1607 by a group of 104 people, as referenced by the National Park Service. The Virginia Company funded the expedition and told the group of settlers to make way for more people to arrive.

Where was the second settlement in North Carolina?
--------------------------------------------------

Sir John Yeamans created a second settlement in North Carolina in 1665 on the Cape Fear River. This is near present-day Wilmington. Charles Town was named the main seat of government in 1670. However, internal problems arose in the colony.

How did the colony of North Carolina get its name?
--------------------------------------------------

The name for the colony was chosen to honor the king. They were given the titles of the Lord Proprietors of the Province of Carolina.
