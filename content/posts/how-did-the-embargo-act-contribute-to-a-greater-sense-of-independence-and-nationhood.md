How did the Embargo Act contribute to a greater sense of independence and nationhood?
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Embargo of 1807 was a major political development that increases nationalistic sentiment. Jefferson’s embargo cut off all trade with foreign countries. This increased the independence of the United States and the nationalistic pride of the citizens.

What did the Embargo Act establish?
-----------------------------------

In 1807 the United States Congress passed an Embargo Act that prohibited American ships from trading in all foreign ports. The act was in response to a dire situation America faced when it found itself caught between a French and British war.

Who created the Embargo Act?
----------------------------

The Embargo Act of 1807 was a law passed by the United State Congress and signed by President Thomas Jefferson on December 22, 1807. It prohibited American ships from trading in all foreign ports.

What did Jefferson’s Embargo do?
--------------------------------

The Embargo Act of 1807 was an attempt by President Thomas Jefferson and the U.S. Congress to prohibit American ships from trading in foreign ports. It was intended to punish Britain and France for interfering with American trade while the two major European powers were at war with each other.

-------------------------------------------------
