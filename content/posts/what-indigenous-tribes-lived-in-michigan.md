Who were the first settlers in Michigan?
----------------------------------------

The first permanent European settlement in Michigan was founded in 1668 at Sault Ste. Marie by Jacques Marquette, a French missionary. The French built several trading posts, forts, and villages in Michigan during the late 17th century.

Who are the three Native American tribes in Michigan?
-----------------------------------------------------

While Michigan was home to many Native American tribes, three important tribes were known as the Three Fires. They were the Ojibwe, the Ottawa, and the Potawatomi. They shared a common culture: similar language, housing, food, trade, and customs.
