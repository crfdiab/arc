What was before Massachusetts?
------------------------------

Prior to 1685 there were two separate colonies within the boundaries of present-day Massachusetts. The area around Plymouth and Cape Cod, settled by the Pilgrims, was known as Plymouth colony, or the Old Colony. The Old Colony was rapidly overshadowed by its Puritan neighbour to the north, the Massachusetts Bay Colony.

Which 2 colonies were once part of Massachusetts?
-------------------------------------------------

Rhode Island and Maine were colonies that were once part of Massachusetts.

Was Maine ever a part of Massachusetts?
---------------------------------------

Maine statehood The Massachusetts General Court passed enabling legislation on June 19, 1819 separating the District of Maine from the rest of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. The following month, on July 26, voters in the district approved statehood by 17,091 to 7,132.

When did Massachusetts become part of the United States?
--------------------------------------------------------

Becoming a State Massachusetts became the sixth state to join the United States on February 6, 1788. John Adams from Boston became the first Vice President and the second President of the United States.

When did the Massachusetts Constitution become a law?
-----------------------------------------------------

Six years before ratification of the United States Constitution in 1789, and 20 years before Marbury v. Madison firmly established the principle of judicial review on a national level in 1803, the Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court recognized the supremacy of the Massachusetts Constitution.

When did slavery become legal in the state of Massachusetts?
------------------------------------------------------------

Massachusetts Constitution and the Abolition of Slavery In 1780, when the Massachusetts Constitution went into effect, slavery was legal in the Commonwealth. However, during the years 1781 to 1783, in three related cases known today as “the Quock Walker case,” the Supreme Judicial Court applied the principle of judicial review to abolish slavery.

What was the history of Massachusetts before the Civil War?
-----------------------------------------------------------

The entire Commonwealth of Massachusetts has played a powerful scientific, commercial, and cultural role in the history of the United States. Before the American Civil War, Massachusetts was a center for the abolitionist, temperance, and transcendentalist movements.
