What kind of government did Spain have after the Civil War?
-----------------------------------------------------------

The Spanish Republic (Spanish: República Española), commonly known as the Second Spanish Republic (Spanish: Segunda República Española), was the form of government in Spain from 1931 to 1939.

-----------------------------------------------

What does Republican mean in Spain?
-----------------------------------

Republicanism in Spain is a political position and movement that holds that Spain should be a Republic. Under the monarchical regime currently in force in Spain, there are movements and political parties throughout the entire political spectrum that advocate for a Third Spanish Republic.

What kind of government did Spain have before the Civil War?
------------------------------------------------------------

Government and society. From 1833 until 1939 Spain almost continually had a parliamentary system with a written constitution. Except during the First Republic (1873–74), the Second Republic (1931–36), and the Spanish Civil War (1936–39), Spain also always had a monarchy.

Do you know about the Spanish Civil War?
----------------------------------------

If you have an interest in Spain, or especially if you love history, it is likely that you’ve heard of the Spanish Civil War. And if you haven’t, that’s ok too. Outside of Spain, and even within, the event is not taught in depth, or at all.

When did Spain return to a constitutional monarchy?
---------------------------------------------------

The Spanish Constitution of 1978 re-established a constitutional monarchy as the form of government for Spain.

Who is the head of government in Spain?
---------------------------------------

The Kingdom of Spain is a parliamentary constitutional monarchy guided by the 1978 constitution. The head of government is the king, a position which is hereditary.
