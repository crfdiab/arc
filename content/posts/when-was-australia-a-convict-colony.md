When was Australia a convict colony?
------------------------------------

January 26, 1788  
On January 26, 1788, Captain Arthur Phillip guides a fleet of 11 British ships carrying convicts to the colony of New South Wales, effectively founding Australia.

When did convicts come to Western Australia?
--------------------------------------------

Forty three convict ships arrived in Western Australia between 1850 and 1868.

When did the first fleet of convicts arrived in Australia?
----------------------------------------------------------

May 13, 1787  
May 13, 1787 CE: ‘First Fleet’ Sets Sail for Australia. On May 13, 1787, the “First Fleet” of military leaders, sailors, and convicts set sail from Portsmouth, England, to found the first European colony in Australia, Botany Bay.

When did the first convicts come to Australia?

Why did the British send convicts to New South Wales?
-----------------------------------------------------

When the British set up the New South Wales colony, they planned to develop the region’s economy by using convict labor on government farms. Other convicts would be “assigned” to work for free settlers. Eventually more than 150,000 convicts were sent to eastern Australia and nearly 10,000 to Western Australia.
