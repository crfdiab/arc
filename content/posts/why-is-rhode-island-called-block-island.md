Why is Rhode Island called Block Island?
----------------------------------------

Their name for the island was “Manisses” which translates to “ Island of the Little God.” In 1614, Adrian Block, a Dutch explorer sailed to the island and gave it his name. He called it Block Island and it was in 1661 that the Island was settled.

When was Block Island incorporated in Rhode Island?
---------------------------------------------------

Block Island was incorporated by the Rhode Island general assembly in 1672, and the island government adopted the name “New Shoreham.” During the War of 1812, the island was briefly occupied by the British Navy under the command of Sir Thomas Hardy.
