What note was sent to Mexico in January 1917 that helped persuade the United States to enter World War I?
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Zimmermann Telegram  
The Zimmermann Telegram was a message sent on January 12, 1917, from the German foreign minister Arthur Zimmerman to the country’s embassy in Washington, D.C., to be relayed to German representatives in Mexico.

What did Germany do to help Mexico in World War 1?
--------------------------------------------------

Germany also made a secret offer to help Mexico regain territories lost in the Mexican–American War in an encoded telegram known as the Zimmermann Telegram, which was intercepted by British intelligence. Publication of that communique outraged Americans just as German U-boats started sinking American merchant ships in the North Atlantic.

What did Mexico get in return for attacking the US?
---------------------------------------------------

In return for attacking the United States, Mexico was promised the return of territory lost during the Mexican-American War (1846-1848), including Texas, New Mexico, and Arizona, as well as substantial financial assistance.

Why did the United States enter World War 1?
--------------------------------------------

The American public was outraged by the news of the Zimmerman telegram and it, along with Germany’s resumption of submarine attacks, helped lead to the U.S. to join the war.
