Does every child in India go to school?
---------------------------------------

Less than half of India’s children between the age 6 and 14 go to school. At least 35 million children aged 6 – 14 years do not attend school. 53% of girls in the age group of 5 to 9 years are illiterate. In India, only 53% of habitation has a primary school.

How does the Indian education system work?
------------------------------------------

The school system in India has four levels: lower primary (age 6 to 10), upper primary (11 and 12), high (13 to 15) and higher secondary (17 and 18). The lower primary school is divided into five “standards”, upper primary school into two, high school into three and higher secondary into two.

How long do Indian kids go to school?
-------------------------------------

Most students begin their schooling at age five, in the form of preschool. By the time they’re six, primary school begins. Students remain obligated to attend school until age 14, at which point they may choose to halt their education.
