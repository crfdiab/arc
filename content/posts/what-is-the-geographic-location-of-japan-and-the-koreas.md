What is the geographic location of Japan and the Koreas?
--------------------------------------------------------

Japan is located just east of the Korean Peninsula across the Korea Strait. The Korean Peninsula is now split between South Korea and North Korea. The capital city of North Korea is Pyongyang, and Seoul is the capital of South Korea.

What are the geographic features of Korea?
------------------------------------------

The country is largely mountainous, with small valleys and narrow coastal plains. The T’aebaek Mountains run in roughly a north-south direction along the eastern coastline and northward into North Korea, forming the country’s drainage divide.

Where are the four main islands of Japan located?
-------------------------------------------------

The four main islands of Japan — Honshū, Hokkaidō, Shikoku and Kyūshū — lie in the Pacific Ocean off the East Asian coast, neighbouring the Koreas, Russia and China just beyond. The beautiful countryside has a backbone of volcanoes and mountains up the islands’ length with flood plains and valleys either side.

How is Japan different from China and Korea?
--------------------------------------------

•At the closest point, the main Japanese islands are 120 miles away from the mainland. •Unlike China and Korea, the borders of Japan have stayed relatively stable throughout most of its history due to its geographic status as a group of islands.
