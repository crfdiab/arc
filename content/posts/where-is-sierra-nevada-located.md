Is the Sierra Nevada in California or Nevada?
---------------------------------------------

The Sierra Nevada ( / siˌɛrə nɪˈvædə, – ˈvɑːdə /) is a mountain range in the Western United States, between the Central Valley of California and the Great Basin. The vast majority of the range lies in the state of California, although the Carson Range spur lies primarily in Nevada.

Where are the cities in the Sierra Nevada mountains?
----------------------------------------------------

On the western side of the Sierra Nevada lies the Central Valley of California and on the east it is bordered by the Basin and Range Province. There are many cities and communities located in various sections of the Sierra Nevada, including South Lake Tahoe, Carson City, Grass Valley and Paradise.

Where does the snow fall in the Sierra Nevada mountains?
--------------------------------------------------------

The name Sierra Nevada comes from Spanish and means “snowy mountain range.” The Sierra gets a lot of snow, particulary in the Central and Northern parts of the range. Most of the snow falls on the western slope of the mountains. That snowfall is the source of most of the drinking water in California.
