Why are rivers important in North America?
------------------------------------------

River and lakes are abundant in North America and they are of great importance. It provides the water for both the industrial as well as agricultural purpose near and around the rivers and lakes. It also plays an important role in regulating the quality of water and in the water cycle process.

What two rivers are in North America?
-------------------------------------

Two rivers, the Colorado and the Rio Grande, begin in the United States and flow into or form a border with Mexico. In addition, the drainage basins of the Mississippi and Missouri rivers extend into Canada, and the basin of the Gila River extends into Mexico.

Why are the Great Lakes in North America?
-----------------------------------------

They are described as Great Lakes because they are some of the most significant natural features of the North American continent, as well as the world. They are a series of freshwater lakes located in the east-central part of North America.

What kind of land is in North America?
--------------------------------------

Much of North America’s land is entirely covered in water, since it borders the Arctic Ocean, Atlantic Ocean, Pacific Ocean, and Caribbean Sea, to the north, east, and southeast, respectively. The land is also covered by many lakes, some of which are among the largest lakes in the world.
