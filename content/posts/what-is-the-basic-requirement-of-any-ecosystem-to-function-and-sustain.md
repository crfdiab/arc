What is the basic requirement of any ecosystem to function and sustain?
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Complete answer: Solar energy is the basic requirement for any ecosystem to function and sustain either its grassland ecosystem or aquatic ecosystem.

What is man made ecosystem?
---------------------------

An ecosystem, which is created and maintained by human beings, is called artificial or man- made ecosystem. Some examples of man- made ecosystem are aquarium, garden, agriculture, apiary, poultry, piggery etc.

What are three ecosystem examples?
----------------------------------

There are aquatic ecosystems such as wetlands, lakes, coral reefs, hydrothermal vents, and others. There are also terrestrial ecosystems such as deserts, savannas, tundras, mountains and the forested ecosystems such as tropical evergreen, tropical deciduous, boreal, and others.

What are the physical and chemical factors of an ecosystem?
-----------------------------------------------------------

These are also called as the ecological factors. The physical and chemical factors are characteristic of the environment. Light, air, soil, and nutrients, etc. form the abiotic components of an ecosystem. The abiotic factors vary from ecosystem to ecosystem.

What are the characteristics of ecosystem based management?
-----------------------------------------------------------

Ecosystem-Based Management includes the following core characteristics: Adaptive management is an essential aspect of EBM; it is a way of managing the dynamic nature of ecosystems in the face of uncertainty by considering a broad range of influences within a region, including external influences, factors, and stressors.

How are abiotic factors vary from ecosystem to ecosystem?
---------------------------------------------------------

The abiotic factors vary from ecosystem to ecosystem. In an aquatic ecosystem, the abiotic factors may include water pH, sunlight, turbidity, water depth, salinity, available nutrients and dissolved oxygen.
