Is it worth going to Loch Lomond?
---------------------------------

Just a stone’s throw from Glasgow lies the enchantingly beautiful Loch Lomond &amp; The Trossachs National Park. Famous for its stunning scenery; the national park offers mountains, lochs, forests and glens across 1,865 square km. There’s so much to see and do in the area, it’s well worth making repeat visits.

Which is the nearest city to Loch Lomond?
-----------------------------------------

The closest cities to Loch Lomond are Stirling to the east and Glasgow to the south east, around 30 minutes away by car. Perth and Edinburgh are both less than 2 hours’ drive. Each of our vibrant Scottish cities is packed with attractions and it can take several days to explore each one.

How long do you need in Loch Lomond?
------------------------------------

With a surface area of 71 sq. km it would take over 20 hours to walk the entire was around the loch. There are a range of trails in the area that would allow you to see much of the loch without taking the 60 mile plus circuit. Discover more Loch Lomond walking guides.

How do you get to Loch Lomond without a car?
--------------------------------------------

Getting to Loch Lomond from Glasgow (without a car!) The easiest way to get to Loch Lomond is to take the train to Balloch. This gateway town to the National Park has many facilities including a visitor center, pubs, hotels and more.

Why does Loch Lomond attract tourists?
--------------------------------------

Loch Lomond &amp; The Trossachs National Park itself attracts millions of visitors every year because of its world-renowned natural beauty, extensive outdoor recreation opportunities, its close proximity to the large population centres of central Scotland and accessibility by road and rail.

Are there seals in Loch Lomond?
-------------------------------

The loch is not only inhabited by seals but by many other sea faring animals and while you are out on the water you may also see the local porpoises playing in the water.

Is Loch Lomond Scotch?
----------------------

Is Loch Lomond in Forth Valley?
-------------------------------

Situated in the centre of Scotland, and stretching almost from coast-to-coast Loch Lomond, the Trossachs, Stirling &amp; the Forth Valley incorporates some of Scotland’s best known sights and is known as the ‘Gateway to the Highlands’.

Do you need a car in Loch Lomond?
---------------------------------

How to explore Loch Lomond without a car. There’s so much to do around the loch right from Balloch Station, there’s really no need for a car. There are plenty of walks all around this southern end of Loch Lomond that make it the perfect place to come on a sunny summer’s day.

Is Loch Lomond free?
--------------------
