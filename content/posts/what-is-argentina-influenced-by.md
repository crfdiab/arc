What is Argentina influenced by?
--------------------------------

Largely influenced by Spanish, Italian and other European backgrounds, modern Argentina is a modge podge of European meets American Indian. This unique blend gives Argentina its distinctive cultural flair.

What European powers settled Argentina?
---------------------------------------

Spain colonized Argentina in the 16th century; it declared its independence in 1816 and emerged as a democratic republic in the mid 19th century, but has since then periodically fallen under military rule.

Where did the German people come from in Argentina?
---------------------------------------------------

German Argentines ( German: Deutschargentinier, Spanish: germano-argentinos) are Argentine citizens of German ancestry. They are descendants of Germans who immigrated to Argentina from Germany and elsewhere in Europe. Some German Argentines originally settled in Brazil, then later immigrated to Argentina.

Why did Argentina support Germany during World War 2?
-----------------------------------------------------

During World War Two, Argentina clearly favored the Axis because of close cultural ties with Germany, Spain, and Italy. This is not surprising, as most Argentines were of Spanish, Italian, or German descent. Nazi Germany nurtured this sympathy, promising important trade concessions after the war.

Who was the first person to settle in Argentina?
------------------------------------------------

The presence of European people in the Argentine territory began in 1516, when Spanish Conquistador Juan Díaz de Solís explored the Río de la Plata. In 1527, Sebastian Cabot founded the fort of Sancti Spiritus, near Coronda, Santa Fe; this was the first Spanish settlement on Argentine soil.

Why did the colonias alemanas come to Argentina?
