Can a waterspout become a tornado?
----------------------------------

Waterspouts are tornado-like columns of water and air that form over water, or have moved to water after forming on land \[source: NOAA\]. If a waterspout moves onshore after forming on water, it technically becomes a tornado \[source: Feltgen\].

Is a waterspout a hurricane?
----------------------------

Just like tornadoes, waterspouts are formed by winds within thunderstorms. Hurricanes provide spin through the well organize cluster of thunderstorms that are spinning around a center. Waterspouts also need, warm, moist air which hurricanes can also provide. They are tornadic waterspouts and fair-weather waterspouts.
