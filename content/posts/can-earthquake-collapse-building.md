Can earthquake collapse building?
---------------------------------

Earthquakes subject buildings to horizontal loads that can result in structural failure and the vertical collapse of a building, or cause non-structural elements of the construction – such as walls—to break off and fall.

What are the main causes of building collapse?
----------------------------------------------

Five reasons why buildings collapse

- The foundations are too weak.
- The building materials aren’t strong enough.
- Workers make mistakes.
- The load is heavier than expected.
- The strength isn’t tested.

-----------------------------------------------------

Can a floor collapse from too much weight?
------------------------------------------

Excessive Weight on a Floor The weight limits for the floor of a building should be considered when the structure is being built. However, if load-bearing supports are not installed properly, this can cause a floor to collapse.

---------------------------------------------------------------

Why do most people die during an earthquake?
--------------------------------------------

Most deaths occur during an earthquake when buildings collapse, which is one reason most countries have strict seismic building codes. Japan is considered a prime example of a country that is earthquake prepared.
