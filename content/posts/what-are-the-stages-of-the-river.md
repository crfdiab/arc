What are the stages of the river?
---------------------------------

Stages of the River

- The course of a river includes the upper stage, the middle stage, and the final stage.
- The upper stage of a river is also called the youthful stage or mountain stage.
- The place where two rivers join is called as the confluence.
- Middle stage is the matured stage of a river.

Does a river speed up or slow down?
-----------------------------------

As the volume of water in a river increases, through smaller streams flowing into it, for example, the velocity of the river increases.

What happens at the end of a river?
-----------------------------------

The current of the river no longer has the force to carry stones or gravel. This material drops to the riverbed, where it forms bars of sand or gravel or builds islands. When the river reaches the end of its journey, it is called an old river. The end of the river is called the mouth.
